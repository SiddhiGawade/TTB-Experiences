01 Points to Remember

Essential cultural etiquette, expert health advice, and local safety precautions for a seamless stay.

General Advice

  • Cash Reliance: Thailand is a cash-reliant society. While cards are accepted at major hotels and malls, carrying Thai Baht (THB) is essential for street food, local markets, and smaller transactions.
  • Temple Dress Codes: When visiting temples or sacred sites, strict dress codes apply: both men and women must cover their shoulders and knees. Carrying a lightweight shawl or sarong is a great way to easily cover up.
  • Shoe Removal Etiquette: You will often need to remove your shoes before entering temples and some local shops.
  • Traditional Greeting: The traditional greeting, the "Wai" (pressing palms together), is a polite gesture to return when greeted.
Expert Doctor's Perspective

Customized Medical & Health Advice

  • Mosquito-Borne Illness Prevention: Dengue fever is a risk, especially around the rainy season; protect yourself by applying DEET-based insect repellent and wearing breathable, long-sleeved clothing, particularly during the day.
  • Water Safety Mandate: Only consume bottled or filtered water.
  • Digestive Health: Eat at busy, reputable eateries to prevent traveler's diarrhea.
  • Hydration & Sun Defense: The tropical sun is intense even on cloudy days, so generous application of high-SPF sunscreen and adequate hydration with electrolyte tablets are crucial to prevent heat exhaustion.
Doctor's Key Note
Keep DEET repellent and electrolyte hydration tablets in your daypack at all times while exploring outdoor sights.
Local Guide's Perspective

Precautionary & Preparation Tips

  • Andaman Sea Rip Currents: The sea can be treacherous in October due to strong rip currents. Always heed beach warning flags.
  • Transport Fare Agreement: If you are taking local transport like tuk-tuks or unmetered taxis, always agree on the exact fare before starting your journey to avoid disputes.
  • Rain Protection: Given unpredictable tropical showers, keeping electronics and valuables in a reliable waterproof dry bag while exploring is a must.
CRITICAL SEA SAFETY WARNING
RED FLAG = ABSOLUTELY NO ENTERING THE WATER. Never ignore red warning flags posted on Phuket beaches in October.

02 Weather & Packing Recommendations

Understand October's climate transition and prepare your wardrobe with precision.

Climate Overview: October Transition Period

Visiting in October means you are arriving during a transition period as the monsoon season begins to subside. It is still one of the wetter months, but you can expect fewer crowds and lush green landscapes.

07 Logistics & Practical Utilities

Connectivity, local transportation options, and recommended mobile applications.

📱
Connectivity & Data

SIM Cards & Mobile Networks

  • Best Networks: AIS and True offer the best coverage and speeds across Phuket.
  • Airport Option: Purchase a tourist SIM card directly at the airport upon arrival (passport required for registration).
  • eSIM Alternative: Use an eSIM service to activate data before landing for immediate connectivity.
🚖
Transport Options

Local Transportation

  • Ride-Hailing: Ride-sharing apps are highly recommended for reliable transport. Grab is widely used; Bolt often offers cheaper rates.
  • Public Bus Option: For budget travel along the coast, the air-conditioned Phuket Smart Bus connects the airport to popular beach towns.
