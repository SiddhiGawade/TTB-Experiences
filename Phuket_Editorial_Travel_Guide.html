Travel Recommendation Guide: Phuket

Visiting Phuket in October offers a unique transition period as monsoon rains subside, revealing lush green landscapes and tranquil beaches with far fewer crowds. Explore health advisories, weather preparation, curated sights, local markets, and dining directories.

1. Points to Remember

Essential local etiquette, doctor's health recommendations, and ocean safety guidance.

LOCAL ETIQUETTE & CASH

General Advice

  • Cash Reliance: Thailand is cash-reliant. While major hotels and malls accept cards, carrying Thai Baht (THB) is essential for street food, local markets, and smaller transactions.
  • Temple Dress Code: Strict rules apply—both men and women must cover shoulders and knees. Carrying a lightweight shawl or sarong allows easy cover-up.
  • Shoe Protocol: You will often need to remove shoes before entering temples and some shops.
  • "Wai" Greeting: The traditional greeting of pressing palms together is a polite gesture to return when greeted.
DOCTOR'S HEALTH ADVICE

Medical & Health Guidance

  • Dengue Prevention: Dengue fever risk is elevated around rainy season. Apply DEET-based insect repellent and wear breathable long sleeves during the day.
  • Safe Hydration: Only consume bottled or filtered water to prevent waterborne illness.
  • Reputable Dining: Eat at busy, reputable eateries to prevent traveler's diarrhea.
  • Sun & Electrolytes: Tropical sun is intense even on cloudy days. Apply high-SPF sunscreen and use electrolyte tablets to prevent heat exhaustion.
LOCAL GUIDE PRECAUTIONS

Safety & Transportation

RED FLAG WARNING = ABSOLUTELY NO SWIMMING
  • Andaman Sea Rip Currents: October currents can be treacherous. Always heed beach flags—a Red Flag means strict no swimming.
  • Fare Pre-Negotiation: If taking tuk-tuks or unmetered taxis, always agree on the exact fare before starting your journey.
  • Waterproof Gear: Given sudden tropical showers, keep electronics and valuables in a reliable waterproof dry bag while exploring.

LOCATION: NEAR PATONG • ACCESS: BOAT OR HIKE

7. Logistics & Practical Info

Essential connectivity, local transportation networks, and recommended digital application suite.

CONNECTIVITY

SIM Cards & Coverage

  • Coverage: AIS and True offer the best network coverage.
  • Airport Purchase: Tourist SIM available at airport upon arrival (Passport required).
  • eSIM Option: Activate data before landing using an eSIM service.
TRANSPORTATION

Local Transit

  • Ride-Hailing: Highly recommended. Grab is widely used; Bolt often offers cheaper rates.
  • Phuket Smart Bus: Air-conditioned budget transit connecting airport to popular beach towns.
DIGITAL SUITE

Essential Apps

  • Grab & Bolt: Safe, transparent ride-hailing.
  • Google Maps: Island navigation.
  • Foodpanda / GrabFood: Food delivery.
  • Google Translate: Overcoming language barriers.
