01  —  Essentials

Points to Remember

Critical tips from a general traveller, medical expert, and local guide perspective — before you land.

General

General Travel Advice

  • Thailand is cash-reliant — always carry Thai Baht (THB) for markets, street food & tuk-tuks.
  • Temple dress code: cover shoulders & knees. Carry a lightweight sarong or shawl.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples and many local shops.
  • Return the "Wai" greeting — press palms together as a polite acknowledgement.

Medical & Health

Doctor's Health Advice

  • Dengue fever risk in rainy season — use DEET-based repellent and wear breathable long sleeves.
  • Drink only bottled or filtered water; eat at busy, reputable eateries.
  • The tropical sun is intense even on cloudy days — apply high-SPF sunscreen generously.
  • Stay hydrated — carry electrolyte tablets to prevent heat exhaustion.

Precautions

Local Guide's Safety Tips

  • October seas can be treacherous — always obey beach warning flags.
  • Agree on tuk-tuk fares before boarding to avoid disputes.
  • Keep electronics in a waterproof dry bag for sudden tropical downpours.

04  —  Hidden Gems

Local Experiences

Go beyond the tourist trail — these are the experiences that will define your trip.

Hidden Gem

Freedom Beach

Tucked away and accessible primarily by boat or a challenging hike, this secluded beach offers a serene escape with powdery white sand — far from the typical tourist crowds. The journey there is half the adventure: the reward is pure, untouched paradise. Perfect for a peaceful morning swim or a picnic on the shore.

Hidden Gem

Ao Sane Beach

A rocky, hidden spot near Nai Harn — excellent for snorkeling and observing vibrant marine life just below the surface.

Thai Culinary Discovery

Take a local cooking class with a Phuket Town Central Market tour. Learn the secrets of authentic Thai cuisine firsthand.

Muay Thai Match

Witness a professional Muay Thai bout at a local stadium — a powerful way to experience a cornerstone of Thai culture and athletic tradition. The atmosphere is electric, raw, and unforgettable.

06  —  Cuisine

Food & Dining

From street-side bowls of Tom Yum to refined South Indian fare — Phuket's culinary scene is extraordinary.

Vegetarian Dishes

Som Tum

Green Papaya Salad

Request it without dried shrimp or fish sauce. Refreshing, tangy, and perfectly spiced.

Pad Thai Je

Vegetarian Pad Thai

The classic Thai noodle dish made entirely without meat or seafood — still deeply flavourful.

Tom Kha Hed

Coconut Mushroom Soup

A rich, creamy coconut broth with mushrooms, lemongrass and galangal. Wonderfully aromatic.

Non-Vegetarian Dishes

Tom Yum Goong

Thailand's most iconic soup — a fiery, sour broth packed with plump prawns and fragrant herbs.

A hearty, mildly spiced curry usually made with beef or chicken — influenced by Persian & Indian flavors.

07  —  Practical

Logistics & Practical Info

Everything you need to stay connected, get around, and navigate Phuket like a pro.

SIM & Connectivity

  • AIS — Best overall network coverage across the island and rural areas.
  • True — Excellent urban coverage; competitive tourist SIM packages.
  • Purchase a tourist SIM at the airport on arrival (bring your passport).
  • Use an eSIM service to activate data before you even land — zero hassle.

Local Transportation

  • Grab — Widely used ride-hailing app. Safe, metered, and reliable.
  • Bolt — Often cheaper than Grab. Good coverage in tourist areas.
  • Phuket Smart Bus — Air-conditioned bus connecting the airport to all major beach towns.
  • Always agree on a fare with tuk-tuk drivers before boarding.
